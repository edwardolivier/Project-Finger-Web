Password Dongle Device Recovery

Device Locked Out

Your dongle wiped itself after five failed fingerprint attempts. All fingerprint templates and passwords have been erased. The device is safe — nothing was leaked — but it needs to be reset and re-enrolled before it can be used again.

You will need to re-enroll all fingers and re-enter your passwords. Your secret key can stay the same or you can choose a new one.

Recovery Steps

Follow these four phases in order. The whole process takes about 10 minutes.

A
Enter BOOTSEL Mode
  1. Unplug the dongle
    Remove it from the USB port completely.
  2. Hold the BOOTSEL button
    Press and hold the small white button on the Raspberry Pi Pico. Keep holding it while you plug the dongle back in.
  3. Confirm the drive appears
    A drive named RPI-RP2 will appear in your file explorer. You can now release the button.
B
Wipe the Flash

This clears any remaining data and firmware from the Pico.

  1. Download flash_nuke.uf2
    Get it from the Raspberry Pi documentation website (search for "flash_nuke.uf2" on raspberrypi.com), or use the copy in your project's 0. reset folder if you have it to hand.
  2. Copy it onto RPI-RP2
    Drag and drop flash_nuke.uf2 onto the RPI-RP2 drive. The Pico will reboot — the drive disappears for a second and then reappears.
C
Reinstall CircuitPython
  1. Get the CircuitPython UF2
    Download the latest CircuitPython 10.x UF2 for Raspberry Pi Pico (RP2040) from circuitpython.org/downloads. Use the copy in your 0. reset folder if you already have one.
  2. Copy it onto RPI-RP2
    Drag the CircuitPython UF2 onto the RPI-RP2 drive. The Pico reboots and a new drive called CIRCUITPY appears.
D
Reinstall Firmware Files
  1. Copy boot.py and code.py
    From your project's firmware/ folder, copy boot.py and code.py to the root of the CIRCUITPY drive.
  2. Copy the lib folder
    From your project's 0. reset/lib/ folder, copy the contents into CIRCUITPY/lib/. You need:
    • adafruit_fingerprint.mpy
    • adafruit_hid/ (full folder)
    • adafruit_bus_device/ (full folder)
  3. Unplug and replug
    The dongle LED should glow yellow, indicating it is in setup mode and ready to be configured again.

Ready to Re-Enrol?

Once the LED is glowing yellow, open the setup wizard to configure your fingers and passwords again.

You can use the same secret key as before, or choose a new one. Either way you will need to re-enter all your passwords.
